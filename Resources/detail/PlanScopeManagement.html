Plan Scope Management is the process of putting together a scope management plan which describes the scope in depth including methods of defining, validating and controlling the scope of the project.

Content gathered from: PMI, A Guide to the Project Management Body of knowledge (PMBOK Guide) Fifth Edition 2013

Inputs

  • Project Management Plan:

    This document explains the execution plan of the project, as well as methods to ensure that the project is monitored and controlled. It includes baselines of the project including the scope, cost and schedule as well as other plans such as the communications plan, requirements management plan, etc. This project management plan is used as an input to create the scope management plan.

  • Project Charter:

    This document serves as a formal authorization of the project by the project sponsor. IT includes any assumptions, clarifications of the client’s needs, high-level requirements, high-level budget, etc. This project charter basically has the context of the project, which is required as an input to create the scope management plan.

  • Enterprise Environmental Factors:

    These factors need to be documented as they have an impact on the scope management plan; these factors include: corporate culture, corporate infrastructure, industry details and conditions, etc.

  • Organizational Process Assets:

    Processes will impact the scope management plan including policies and procedures, corporate history, and lessons learnt from past projects.

Outputs

  • Scope Management Plan:

    This output is one component of the project management plan that describes the scope of the project in depth: its definition as well as how it will be developed, monitored, controlled and validated. Depending on the project, this step can be formal or informal; either case, it addresses the various processes around the scope management.

  • Requirements Management Plan:

    This output is another component of the project management plan that outlines how the requirements will be analyzed, documented and managed.The project manager decides on a phase-to-phase relationship and bases the requirements management plan on it. It also outlines different processes and how each will be managed such as configuration management, prioritization of requirements, etc.

Tools and Techniques

  • Expert Judgement:

    The input of experts in a knowledge area is a necessary tool in scope management plans. This includes education, previous work experience, training, etc.

  • Meetings:

    Meetings with the project team can be held in order to create the scope management plan. Attendees can include the project manager, sponsor, team members, and other stakeholders that would be able to provide input for the scope management plan.

Glossary

Plan Scope Management
This is the process of putting together a scope management plan which describes the scope in depth including methods of defining, validating and controlling the scope of the project.

Project Charter
This is a formal document created by the project sponsor and serves as the authorization of the project.

Project Scope Management
This includes multiple steps which are meant to ensure that the project meets the requirements, and only its requirements (no more, no less).

Project Sponsor
This person is responsible and accountable for the project and its success. He or she provides support to the rest of the team. It can also be a group of people.

Requirement
A feature or condition that is required in the project in order to satisfy the client’s expectations, as per the scope of the project.
